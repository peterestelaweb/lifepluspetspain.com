## Context

The project already has a Spanish LifePlus Pets landing page, a guide page, OpenSpec campaign context, and a published PETS Outreach CRM. The CRM supports categories such as veterinary clinic and pet store, owner assignment, response status, pipeline stages, next steps, legal-basis metadata, history, CSV import, and CSV export.

Care & Comfort is already in the recommended Phase 1 launch scope. Its official product page describes it as a dog-only spray for pulgas, garrapatas, mosquitos, moscas, acaros, piojos, and other external parasites, with a non-toxic composition, essential oils, use every 2-3 days or after getting wet, and optional use on bedding and grooming tools.

The new opportunity comes from a public health conversation around pesticide exposure. The ScienceDirect article shared by the user is a 2026 nested case-control study on prenatal neonicotinoid insecticide exposure, maternal steroid hormone levels, and precocious puberty in girls. It is relevant as context because some anti-parasite collars use compounds such as imidacloprid, but it is not direct evidence that pet collars cause precocious puberty. AEMPS veterinary safety material also documents practical precautions around some medicated collars, especially avoiding repeated/prolonged contact and limiting child contact. This supports a "reduce unnecessary exposure where possible" conversation, not a medical-causality campaign.

Stakeholders:

- B2C: dog owners, especially families, pregnant women, homes with children, rural/walking-heavy dog owners, and customers already interested in natural wellness.
- B2B: veterinary clinics, pet-food shops, groomers, trainers, dog daycares, breeders, shelters, canine clubs, and local dog communities.
- Internal operators: Peter, Maika, Monica, Belen, and any uplines using the CRM.

## Goals / Non-Goals

**Goals:**

- Make Care & Comfort the lead commercial "hook" for the next LifePlus Pets wave.
- Build a message system that is strong enough to sell but disciplined enough to avoid legal or scientific overreach.
- Convert the public concern into a practical household question: "Can I protect my dog while reducing unnecessary toxic load in the home?"
- Create a B2B list-building and outreach plan for veterinarians and pet-sector businesses.
- Use the existing CRM as the execution layer for prospect capture, segmentation, outreach, and follow-up.
- Produce assets that can be reused across landing, guide, social, email, SMS, WhatsApp, and professional materials.

**Non-Goals:**

- Do not claim that collars, neonicotinoids, or specific brands cause precocious puberty.
- Do not present Care & Comfort as a medicine, veterinary substitute, disease treatment, or guaranteed parasite-control solution.
- Do not create fear-based campaigns aimed at mothers or children.
- Do not scrape or store unnecessary personal data.
- Do not replace veterinary prescription antiparasitic protocols where a vet considers them necessary.

## Decisions

### 1. Positioning: "proteccion cotidiana sin carga toxica innecesaria"

Use Care & Comfort as a proactive comfort-and-repellent routine for dogs, not as an anti-collar attack. The strongest central message is:

`Proteccion externa para perros, con una rutina botanica que ayuda a mantener pulgas, garrapatas y otros parasitos externos bajo control sin cargar el hogar con collares medicados cuando no hacen falta.`

Rationale:

- It names the real job: external parasites.
- It speaks to the household concern: unnecessary toxic load.
- It avoids direct disease/endocrine causality claims.
- It keeps the door open for veterinarians: the product complements responsible routines; it does not replace professional advice.

Alternatives considered:

- "La alternativa segura a los collares toxicos": stronger but too aggressive and legally fragile.
- "Protege a tus hijos de los pesticidas": emotionally powerful but unacceptable because it implies direct human health protection.
- "Antiparasitario natural": useful but too generic and may trigger regulatory ambiguity if not framed carefully.

### 2. Evidence framework: three layers

Every content item must be assigned to one evidence layer:

- Product facts: official Care & Comfort facts, ingredients, mode of use, dog-only limitation, reapplication cadence, use on bedding/tools.
- Context facts: public concerns around pesticides, neonicotinoids, collar contact precautions, children, pregnancy, and household exposure.
- Brand interpretation: LifePlus Pets offers a practical routine for owners who want protection with a gentler everyday profile.

Rationale:

- The study is valuable for timing and education, but not enough to make product-substitution medical claims.
- The AEMPS collar precautions are legitimate context for discussing contact hygiene and household habits.
- Separating facts from interpretation gives social, landing, and sales scripts a reusable compliance structure.

### 3. Claim map

Approved claims:

- Helps control external parasites such as fleas, ticks, mosquitoes, flies, mites, lice, and others.
- Uses natural oils including lemongrass, cinnamon, andiroba, and castor oil.
- Helps soothe mild irritation from bites while supporting skin and coat comfort.
- Can be reapplied every 2-3 days, after wetting, or daily if needed.
- Can be used on dog bedding and grooming tools for an extra layer of routine care.
- Dog-only product.
- Designed for owners looking for a more comfortable, non-toxic external-care routine.

Use with caution:

- "No toxico" only when tied to the official product wording, not expanded into universal safety claims.
- "Alternativa" only as "una opcion para valorar" or "una rutina diferente", not as "sustituto medico".
- "Familias con ninos" only in the context of reducing contact with medicated collars, not as a health outcome promise.
- "Embarazo" only as "consulta con tu profesional sanitario/veterinario y evita contacto innecesario con productos medicados", not as a direct product claim.

Prohibited claims:

- Prevents precocious puberty, endocrine disruption, cancer, infertility, or hormonal harm.
- Collars cause disease in children or pregnant women.
- Care & Comfort is safer than every collar or every veterinary antiparasitic.
- Guaranteed no fleas/ticks.
- Replaces veterinary treatment, prescription protocols, or parasite control in high-risk areas.
- Safe for cats. The official page says dog-only.

### 6. Outreach cadence

Day 0: Add qualified contact to CRM with source and segment.

Day 1: First contact by WhatsApp/SMS/email depending on available public channel.

Day 3: Follow-up 1 if no response. Use a softer educational angle, not a pushy sales angle.

Day 7: Follow-up 2 with one useful asset: one-pager, FAQ, or short landing link.

Day 14: Move to nurture if no response. No repeated cold pressure.

- Offer short call or sample discussion.
- Log objections in CRM: efficacy, veterinary fit, margin, safety, price, customer demand.
- Set next step within 48 hours.

## Risks / Trade-offs

- Scientific overreach -> Use a strict claim map and keep the study as context, not proof of collar harm.
- Fear marketing backlash -> Lead with household comfort and responsible choice, not danger language.
- Veterinary resistance -> Say "consulta a tu veterinario" and position the spray as an everyday care option, not a replacement for clinical parasite protocols.
- Regulatory ambiguity -> Avoid disease-prevention language and do not call it a medicine.
- Product misuse -> Repeat "solo perros", avoid eyes/nose/mouth, use cloth for face, reapply after wetting.
- CRM privacy risk -> Store only public business contact data and outreach notes; avoid sensitive personal or animal medical details.
- List quality decay -> Require source URL/date and retention review.
