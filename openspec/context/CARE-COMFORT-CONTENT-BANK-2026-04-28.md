# Care & Comfort Content Bank

## Objective

Create a set of reusable angles for social posts, email, LinkedIn, and landing copy around Care & Comfort without crossing into unsupported medical or alarmist claims.

## Core Message

`Reduce unnecessary exposure in the home while keeping your dog in a clear external-parasite routine.`

## B2C Angles

1. `What is on your dog's collar and how close is it to the family every day?`
2. `A simpler external-care routine for dogs that live indoors and outdoors`
3. `What Care & Comfort contains and how to use it correctly`
4. `How to build a low-friction routine after walks and before bedtime`
5. `Why some families prefer to reduce unnecessary chemical load in shared spaces`

## B2B Angles

1. `A practical product for clinics and shops that want a dog-only external-care option`
2. `How to explain product use without fear-based selling`
3. `Why some clients ask for alternatives to medicated collars`
4. `A compliant way to talk about external-parasite season`
5. `When a customer wants comfort, routine, and a lower-contact household profile`

## Safe Hook Lines

- `External parasite care without unnecessary load in the home`
- `A dog-only routine spray for daily comfort`
- `A practical option for families asking for a gentler routine`
- `Reduce contact, keep the routine`
- `Simple use, clear ingredients, dog-only`

## Topics To Develop Later

- product ingredient explainer
- how to use on coat, bedding, and grooming tools
- household routine after walks
- vet-facing explanation sheet
- FAQ about dogs, children, and cohabiting pets

## Lines To Avoid

- `collars are toxic`
- `this prevents hormone problems`
- `safe for all pets`
- `works better than every veterinary product`
- `protects children from pesticides`

## Next Production Step

Turn the strongest 3 B2C angles and 3 B2B angles into:

- one carousel
- one reel script
- one LinkedIn post
- one email for clinics
- one FAQ block for the website
